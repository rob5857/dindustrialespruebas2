Why you should NOT clean soot yourself

 Before touching anything, read this:

  • It affects your insurance claim: If you clean before the adjuster documents the damage, they may argue it was minor. You could lose thousands of dollars in your claim.

Frequently Asked Questions

Questions about smoke and soot cleanup

What is soot and why is it dangerous?
Soot is a mixture of carbon, chemicals, and toxic particles produced by incomplete combustion. It penetrates walls, ducts, and fabrics. It is corrosive and can cause serious respiratory problems if not handled correctly by professionals.
Why shouldn't I clean smoke damage before calling insurance?
If you clean before documenting the damage, the insurance adjuster may argue it was minor or non-existent. Additionally, using incorrect products can permanently set the soot. Document first, clean after — with professionals.
How long do invisible smoke damages last?
Acidic smoke gases continue damaging metals, plastics, and paint for days or weeks after a fire. Smoke odor can persist for years if not treated correctly. Quick intervention significantly reduces the total cost.
What techniques do you use to eliminate smoke odor?
We use a combination of ozone treatment, thermal fogging, specific sealants, and positive pressure ventilation. The method depends on the type of materials affected and the extent of the damage.
