Frequently Asked Questions

Questions about fire damage restoration

How long does fire damage restoration take?
It depends on the extent of the damage. Minor damage can be resolved in days; structural damage may take several weeks. We begin the evaluation the same day you call us and give you a realistic estimate from the start.
Does my homeowner's insurance cover fire damage?
In most cases, yes. Standard homeowner's policies in Puerto Rico cover fire damage, smoke, soot, and water used to extinguish the fire. We review your policy with you during the free inspection.
Is it safe to enter my house after a fire?
No. Soot particles, toxic gases, and structurally compromised areas pose serious risks. Do not enter without authorization from firefighters and without proper protective equipment. Call us first — we'll handle the safe assessment.
Why shouldn't I clean the soot myself?
Cleaning before documenting the damage may jeopardize your insurance claim — adjusters may argue the damage was minor. Also, using incorrect products can permanently set soot into surfaces. Document first, then let professionals handle the cleanup.
