FAQ

Questions about water damage restoration

How long do I have to act after a flood?
Time is critical. Mold can start growing in 24 to 48 hours in Puerto Rico's humid climate. Every hour water remains in the structure increases damage and restoration cost. Call us immediately.
Does insurance cover flood damage?
It depends on your policy. Most homeowner's policies cover internal water damage (burst pipes, appliance failures). External flood damage may require a separate NFIP policy. We review your coverage for free.
How do I know if there's mold in my property?
Mold isn't always visible. Warning signs include musty odor, dark or greenish stains on walls and ceilings, deteriorating drywall or wood. We do a complete assessment with specialized equipment.
Can you save my furniture and personal belongings?
Many items can be restored if you act quickly. Within the first 24-48 hours it's possible to save wood furniture, electronics, documents, and clothing. We assess each item individually at no extra cost.
